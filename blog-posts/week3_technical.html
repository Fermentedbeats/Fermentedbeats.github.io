JavaScript: What's the Hype?


Date: September 11, 2014

I'm 5 days deep into Javascript, so let's dig in:

Why JavaScript?

  • Like it or not, JS is here to stay (and of course when it comes to technology "stay" equates to "near-future.") It is a universal language for browsers. I learned HTML & CSS because you simply cannot have a website without them. Same goes for JavaScript: you cannot make cool interactive stuff happen on your website without Javascript (and/or one of it's descendants).
  • Type in "page abandonment + load time" into your search engine. There are mountains of competing data out there, but it all boils down the same way: the longer it takes something to load, the faster your potential customer/sale/visitor clicks away, never to return. The trend predictably shows "even faster" as the cry of the masses. JavaScript is on-board. Because JS interacts with the DOM (Document Object Model), everything is done on the downstream of the web server. No server-side processing = faster load times.

But, but, but...

  • Google "Javascript sucks" to see just under 8 million matches. There's even a Facebook page. With my rudimentary knowledge I'm personally leery of JS' willingness to convert numbers to characters and make best guesses, the lack of a hash syntax, and the heavy semicolon seasoning. Nonetheless, it's been easy to learn so far compared to starting with Ruby or even HTML5. Plus the miniscule amount of available functions makes it seem manageable. While I do not how JS' lack of a standard library, module system, or OOP will screw me, the internet assures me it's only a matter of time. With the overwhelming JavaScript hatred on the web and in-person, I assume we'll see a replacement in the next few years. My money's on Google's Dart.
