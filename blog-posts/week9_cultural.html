Questions: Ask 'Em


Date: October 22, 2014

Ever thought about what makes a question good? The bad ones are obvious: you know a dud when it wastes your time, is convoluted, or the asker just isn't listening to the answer.


On the brink of Phase 1 at Dev Bootcamp, we're asked to ponder what makes a good question. Let's dig in:

  1. Didja Google it?
    • Don't ask someone what you can look up. Seriously, don't do it.
  2. Really, Google it.
    • See above. Google it. Google it again. Look in a book. Check your notes.
  3. Talk it out.
    • Get your thoughts together before you ask for help. Vocalizing a problem taps into other areas of your brain. Try speaking out loud.
  4. Get shorty.
    • Can you ask your question in 3 sentences or less? Good, now make it 1 sentence.
  5. Have props.
    • Have examples ready of where things are going wrong.
  6. Listen.
    • You might get an answer that's way to intense and fast for you to understand. That's okay, just listen, and keep asking questions until you are on the same page.
  7. Gratitude
    • Maybe you didn't get an answer. Or perhaps you still have no way to solve your problem. Look, you still took up someone's time. Be appreciative.



How do you ask questions? Send me an email and tell me about it.
